The frame

60 / 30 / 10. Applied across the portfolio.

Most organizations automate the wrong things. About sixty percent of most AI-adjacent problems are traditional code and database work. About thirty percent are rule-based logic. About ten percent are genuine AI problems. Founders who skip that triage burn runway putting large language models where a well-scoped database query would be faster, cheaper, and auditable.

60% Traditional code

Database work, plumbing, state management. A well-written query beats an LLM call every time.

30% Rule-based logic

Deterministic flows, compliance gates, decision trees. Auditable. Cheap. No hallucinations.

10% Genuine AI

Orchestration-layer work where inference is the product. This is where the moat actually lives.

Applied across a portfolio of veteran-led technology companies, the 60/30/10 read surfaces two groups. The first is companies whose moat actually depends on AI at the orchestration layer: Havoc's maritime autonomy, Proteus's design-to-launch compression, TERN's GPS-denied navigation. The second is companies where AI is a credibility surface layered over a traditional-software product.

Telling the two apart is the difference between an LP narrative that holds up under diligence and one that does not.
